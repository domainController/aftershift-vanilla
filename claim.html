  • Definition: A position paper is a concise document that clearly states a position on a specific issue. It typically includes:
    • an analysis of the facts,
    • a justified stance,
    • recommendations or future perspectives.
  • Contexts of use:
    • Diplomatic and political: to express the stance of a state or party on a treaty or conflict.
    • Business and NGOs: to position publicly on controversial issues (climate, labor, social justice, etc.).
    • Academia: often part of legal, political science, or economics coursework.
    • Advocacy journalism: in activist blogs or union media to defend causes.
  • Translations: Position Note or Position Document.
    Sometimes also: Engaged Analysis, Critical Brief, or Public Stand.
  • Relevance for this blog: Very high. It doesn‚Äôt just narrate; it takes a stand on a social or legal dysfunction. This hybrid format combines analysis, testimony, grievance, and methodical breakdown.

  • Definition: An editorial framework is a writing and publishing framework that defines:
    • the editorial intent of the site,
    • its methodology (tone, structure, form),

    • and the reading grid suggested to the audience.
  • Contexts of use:
    • Journalistic environments: to define the editorial line and logic.
    • Legal or documentary platforms: to clarify scope and method for critical readers.

The Unsocial Hours is a tactical-legal journal born out of systematic communication failure. From the first to the last shift, the employment relationship was marked by lack of transparency, lack of guidance, and the absence of any formal framework.

This ongoing blog documents and analyzes a workplace where legitimate requests for clarity or payment were increasingly met with silence or hostility.

The journal was initiated following a dishwasher dismissal without a written contract, and remains active.

It also provides a structured timeline of events, highlights contractual breaches and administrative irregularities, and reviews the legal obligations potentially violated.

The approach combines factual narration, legal interpretation, and socio-professional reflection, to identify broader structural failures and accountability gaps.

This blog functions as a public legal and editorial framework, anchored in labor law terminology and socio-professional analysis. It is also a space of personal training and experimentation ‚Äî an initiation into legal reasoning, narrative structure, and stylistic clarity.

Through this evolving exercise, it seeks to clarify damages, surface deeper conflict mechanisms, and outline actionable legal paths.

The Unsocial Hours also serves as a pragmatic space for defense, documentation, and strategic anticipation in the face of opaque or hostile work environments.

It seeks to echo the voice of those in the shadows of the labor market, especially in roles marked by high turnover, low visibility, or informal contracts ‚Äî such as dishwashing and other back-of-house positions.
