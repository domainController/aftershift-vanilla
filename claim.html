This platform documents how I actually look at companies when considering acquisition. No consulting bullshit, no theoretical frameworks borrowed from business school. Just pattern recognition applied to real operational dysfunction and hidden value spots that most buyers miss.

Financial statements are fiction. What matters is who really makes decisions, where the bottlenecks actually are, and which operational fixes would unlock the most cash. This isn't about perfect due diligence ‚Äî it's about seeing what's broken and knowing how to fix it.

Authority Mapping ‚Äî Who Actually Runs This Thing

Org charts lie. The real question is who actually decides when something goes wrong at 2 PM on a Tuesday. I trace decision patterns backwards from operational failures to find the actual power structure. Most companies have shadow hierarchies where the person with the title isn't the person solving problems.

Authority gaps create cash leaks. When nobody owns a decision, money disappears through delayed responses, duplicated work, and strategic procrastination. Finding these gaps early means knowing exactly where to apply pressure during operational restructuring.

I map three layers: formal authority (what the documents say), operational authority (who people actually call), and crisis authority (who shows up when everything breaks). The gaps between these three layers predict exactly where value is being destroyed.

Operational Bottleneck Detection

Every company has exactly three to five operational bottlenecks that limit growth. Finding them requires watching how information and decisions flow under stress. I focus on timing patterns rather than process documentation ‚Äî when things slow down, where they accumulate, and which person or system consistently becomes the constraint.

Most bottlenecks aren't technical. They're psychological ‚Äî someone afraid to make decisions, someone hoarding information, or someone who's become indispensable by making themselves a required approval step. These human bottlenecks often hide the biggest value creation opportunities because they're usually fixable with simple authority redistribution.

Resource allocation follows bottlenecks. If decision-making is slow, people waste time preparing for meetings that don't matter. If information flow is constrained, teams duplicate work or make decisions with incomplete data. Finding the bottleneck tells you exactly where efficiency gains will compound.

Cash Flow Reality Check

Reported numbers reflect accounting decisions, not operational reality. I trace actual cash movements by looking at payment timing, vendor relationships, and working capital patterns. Companies with clean books but messy operations often have hidden cash optimization opportunities worth more than their asking price.

Operational improvements compound through cash cycles. Fix inventory turnover and working capital improves. Fix decision speed and customer satisfaction improves. Fix authority clarity and both operational efficiency and employee retention improve. Most buyers focus on revenue growth when cash generation efficiency offers better returns with lower risk.

Documentation Method

Everything gets documented in real-time during analysis. Pattern recognition improves with data volume ‚Äî the more companies I analyze, the faster I spot recurring dysfunction types. Each case study feeds back into methodology refinement and helps identify which operational fixes translate across industries.

This isn't academic research. It's pattern library development for practical application. Every documented analysis becomes a reference point for future investment decisions and operational improvement strategies.
