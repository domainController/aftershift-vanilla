Upcoming Publications

Last updated: Thursday, May 1, 2025 at 02:19 AM

This section will soon feature a handful of articles. Each release will consist of two distinct parts: a detailed chronological reconstruction of events and an autonomous legal reflection focusing on applicable laws, available remedies, and broader systemic issues.

No more than three pairs will be published at this stage—six articles maximum. The objective is to explore formats, refine editorial tone, and prepare for a future advanced platform.

First Article: On Dismissal by Text (Om uppsägning via SMS)

Across industries, from anonymous workers to famous CEOs and professional sports coaches, dismissals have often adopted the form of a coward elegance: swift, discreet, and elegantly detached, avoiding the direct confrontation of a traditional face-to-face dismissal, which though painful, retains a certain nobility.

These practices reflect less a conscious cruelty than a blend of urgent pragmatism and efficient contempt, treating individuals not as unique beings but as disposable assets — echoing, perhaps, the raw cynicism embedded in the term "Human Resources."

Drawing from personal experience and broader examples, the articles will examine:

  • What constitutes a valid resignation?
  • What is the purpose of a notice period?
  • How can an employment contract be terminated?
  • Is a friendly procedure legally acceptable?
  • What concrete remedies are available to contest such dismissals?

Each article pair will serve as an exercise in style and structure, setting the foundations for the editorial DNA of the future platform.

The first article is nearing completion and will be released when the foundations feel right.

In Conclusion

As Friedrich Nietzsche once wrote, "No one lies as much as an indignant man."

Rather than indulging in outrage, these articles aim to observe, document, and reflect calmly on realities that have long been part of the contemporary employment landscape.

This current site is a foundational workspace: a place to establish methods, define themes, and set structural baselines before transitioning to a more advanced, writing-focused platform.

Thank you.
