How to read this section

This is the third entry in a triptych exploring the practical, interpersonal, and structural conditions of a workplace conflict. It complements Context and Working Dynamics

This final part focuses on role expectations, managerial presence, and internal authority patterns — especially when formal contracts fail to reflect operational reality.

In corporate sociology or organizational diagnostics, this type of narrative aligns with a role-mapping audit or a governance ambiguity assessment.

It helps clarify not only who was meant to lead, but also who actually did — and where the gaps, silences, or reversals emerged.
