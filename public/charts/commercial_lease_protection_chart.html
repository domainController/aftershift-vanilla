🏪 Commercial Lease Legal Protection

Level of tenant protection for restaurateurs in Europe (2024-2025)

📊 Protection Scale (0-10)

0-3: Minimal protection - Landlord can freely increase rent or evict without major constraints
4-6: Moderate protection - Some legal protections but significant landlord power
7-10: Strong protection - Binding legal framework, facilitated renewal, rent control
Country Score Main Legal Characteristics
🇬🇧United Kingdom 2 Extensive contractual freedom, no automatic renewal right (except limited Landlord and Tenant Act 1954), facilitated evictions
🇵🇱Poland 3 Minimal protection, liberal market, little commercial rent control
🇸🇪Sweden 4 Limited indirect protection, possible compensation (1 year rent) if unjustified eviction, free indexation
🇳🇴Norway 5 Typical 5-10 year leases, mandatory notice, possibility of negotiated renewal
🇫🇮Finland 5 System similar to other Nordic countries, moderate protection
🇩🇰Denmark 6 Commercial Rent Act 2022, regulated termination conditions, mandatory notice periods
🇩🇪Germany 6 Reasonable protection, 3-9 month notice depending on duration, limited eviction grounds
🇪🇸Spain 7 3% increase cap (2024), extraordinary extensions for vulnerable tenants
🇮🇹Italy 7 Tenant-friendly laws, limited termination, regulated rate contracts available
🇧🇪Belgium 8 9-year commercial leases, renewal rights, substantial eviction compensation
🇫🇷France 9 9-year commercial lease, automatic renewal right, mandatory eviction compensation, capped increases (10%/year max)

🔍 Methodology

This evaluation is based on the following criteria:

  • Right to renewal: Automatic, negotiated or non-existent
  • Rent control: Caps on annual increases
  • Eviction compensation: Amount and conditions
  • Minimum lease duration: Protection against precarious leases
  • Eviction grounds: Restrictions imposed on landlords
  • Mandatory notice periods: Tenant protection timeframes

Sources: Legal research 2024-2025 - National commercial codes, comparative analysis of European commercial lease rights, UK Law Commission reports, current national legislation.

Note: Scores are indicative and based on comparative analysis of current legal frameworks. Situations may vary according to local jurisdictions and specific contractual agreements.
