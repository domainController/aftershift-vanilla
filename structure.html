Entities

What is this section?

This section outlines the organizational and structural anatomy of the employer entity. It maps the visible and latent hierarchies that shaped the experience of work, including overlaps between restaurant, hotel, external ownership, and informal delegation.

Depending on the context, this may resemble a corporate organigram (business), a chain of command (logistics), or a liability map (legal accountability). It provides structural clarity to support later claims.

Many terms here—such as proxy management, shadow delegation, or contractual opacity—will be indexed in the glossary.

Quan is a restaurant, but not a registered company. A search in the Swedish company registry yields no results under that name. It appears to function as a brand, not a legal entity.

Upon arrival, I was directed to enter through the Elite Hotel. Quan operates physically within the hotel premises. No signage, uniform, or internal communication displayed its own corporate identity.

The domain quanbyquan.se was registered on 24 August 2018 and remains active to this day (over six years). WHOIS records reveal no owner identity. No legal entity or impressum is mentioned on the site.

Swedish law (Act 2002:562) requires e-commerce and hospitality sites to disclose their operator. The Quan site contains no VAT number, company name, or legal contact.

Using the Wayback Machine, over 45 snapshots from 2018–2024 were reviewed. No version ever included legal information, reinforcing the structural opacity of the domain's ownership.

Despite this, the domain quanbyquan.se is used for professional email communication. Three addresses were verified as active: hs@, sc@, and hk@quanbyquan.se.

These addresses correspond to Henrik Siebert, Sahar El Nasser, and Alexander Saahil Chudasama. All of them confirmed receipt of a formal letter in January 2025.

Weeks into the job, I discovered the restaurant was operated by HSSC Event AB, not Elite Hotel. This became visible when I accessed the internal planning platform Personalkollum.

HSSC Event AB was incorporated on 25 June 2018 by Alexander Saahil Chudasama. Its registration number is 559163-2764. Sahar El Nasser is listed as an alternate board member (suppleant).

The company name HSSC Event AB does not formally list Henrik Siebert among its board members. However, the initials HSSC match the combination of Henrik Siebert (HS) and Sahil Chudasama (SC), two individuals closely tied to the project. This strongly suggests that the name was derived from their identities.

According to the Swedish ownership register, Lin Daniella Dahl holds between 50–75% of HSSC Event AB via KEHDA Holding AB. She is not referenced on the restaurant's website or in internal documents.

Chudasama Invest AB, owned by Alexander Chudasama, holds less than 25% of shares. However, he has the right to appoint and dismiss the majority of board members.

This governance clause gives Alexander effective control of the company, even without majority ownership. His presence is confirmed by field reports and internal emails.

Henrik Siebert does not appear in official company documents. His email under the domain suggests a non-registered but active role in Quan’s operations.

Sahar El Nasser, although not listed as an owner, was the only managerial figure on site. Her status as suppleant indicates a formal link without legal authority over company direction.

In June 2024, a new entity named Tributum AB was incorporated under organisation number 559488-5476. It shares the same address as Quan and HSSC Event AB: Mäster Johansgatan 15, Malmö, the location of the Elite Hotel.

The company lists Henrik Kei Siebert as verkställande direktör (external CEO), Sahar El Nasser as ledamot (board member), and Lin Daniella Dahl as suppleant (board deputy). These appointments form a coherent triangle of decision-making involving the same individuals previously connected to Quan.

Daniella Dahl is officially registered at the same residential address in Trelleborg as Henrik Siebert. This may indicate a domestic or familial connection, though this is not confirmed in official company filings. Their shared involvement in the company strengthens the perception of a jointly coordinated initiative.

Tributum AB was registered with a clear declared business activity: restaurangverksamhet (restaurant operations). This represents a contrast with the structurally opaque profile of HSSC Event AB, which never publicly disclosed its corporate purpose nor maintained a visible legal imprint on Quan’s communications.

Despite this formal declaration, Tributum AB has never issued payslips or registered any employment documents with the HRAK unemployment fund, at least in relation to the complainant. It remains a dormant entity on paper, showing no revenue, no registered staff, and no known operations to date.

Whether Tributum AB was designed to replace, absorb, or complement HSSC Event AB is still unclear. However, the continuity in address, management, and declared purpose strongly suggests that these entities are structurally interconnected. Their combined presence within the same premises indicates a coordinated ecosystem managed by the same group of individuals.

Although Alexander Sahil Chudasama is officially recorded as founder and lead board member of HSSC Event AB, he was never present on site during the entire period I worked at Quan. His absence was consistent and total, despite his formal status as the person holding the company’s registration and signing authority.

Alexander Chudasama is reportedly active at another restaurant location elsewhere in Malmö, suggesting that his operational involvement at Quan was outsourced or delegated entirely. This further supports the impression of a layered governance system, where legal responsibility is separated from daily managerial presence.

Two chefs, Robin Albay Dinopol and Satoru Yoshizawa, who were regularly scheduled on evening shifts, were later identified as the sole partners of BURI Handelsbolag (org.nr 969798-6447). Their company is not listed as a subcontractor, but they both worked onsite as cooks.

This configuration raises the possibility of undeclared subcontracting, where staff are employed through a third-party vehicle without formal declaration. No document or contract was disclosed to clarify the terms of their engagement with Quan or HSSC Event AB.

No public record directly links Elite Hotels and HSSC Event AB. However, both companies officially operate from the same address: Mäster Johansgatan 15, Malmö. This overlap may indicate a tenancy or subcontracting arrangement, but the absence of published contracts prevents confirmation.

Another external subcontractor was regularly observed on site every Friday night. A cleaning couple arrived at approximately 23:30 and remained on site until around 04:00, working four hours each.

Their presence accounted for a total of eight hours per session. They were not listed on any official schedule, and no company name or contractual arrangement was ever disclosed. Management suggested I might be asked to replace them due to dissatisfaction with their work.

Separately, the employment certificate issued in my name was signed by Sahar El Nasser. This formal act establishes her involvement in administrative and personnel matters, beyond her nominal board position.

Despite being officially registered only as a suppleant (alternate board member), her signature confirms an operational role in human resources. It also implies her position as the primary interface with external entities such as HRAK.
