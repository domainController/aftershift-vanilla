+      
+        <p style="line-height: 1.6em">
+          A <strong>holding company</strong> is a legal entity that owns shares in one or more other companies, typically to control or consolidate their operations. It <u>does not necessarily operate a commercial business</u> of its own. In Sweden, this is often used for:  
+        </p>
+      
+        <ul style="line-height: 1.6em">
+          <li>📂 Grouping ownership of multiple subsidiaries under one entity</li>
+          <li>💼 Receiving dividends or profit transfers from operating companies</li>
+          <li>💰 Facilitating strategic financial operations (e.g., capital injections, risk insulation)</li>
+          <li>🧾 Tax structuring — using intercompany loans, transfers, or consolidation of accounts</li>
+        </ul>
+      
+        <p style="line-height: 1.6em">
+          While this structure can offer <strong>efficiency and transparency</strong> in large groups (e.g., a restaurant group with multiple brands), it can also be <span class="highlight">misused for opacity</span>, especially when there is:
+        </p>
+      
+        <ul style="line-height: 1.6em">
+          <li>❌ No real operational subsidiaries under the holding</li>
+          <li>❌ No declared ownership in other companies</li>
+          <li>❌ Distributions of profits without a traceable origin of revenue</li>
+        </ul>
